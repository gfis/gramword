Coding of Morphem Descriptors

The grammatical type of words is coded with a string of abbreviations for various syntactical and semantic features:

  1. Word Class (main attribute)
    • Ab - abbreviation (substantive)
    • Aj - adjective
    • Ar - article (determined)
    • Au - article (undetermined)
    • Av - adverb
    • Cc - concatenation
    • Er - error, invalid spelling
    • Ex - reference to correct entry (e.g. for form with expanded umlauts)
    • Ir - interrogative
    • Nm - name
    • Nu - number
    • Pn - pronoun
    • Pr - preposition
    • Rp - reply or exclamation
    • Sb - substantive
    • Un - unspecified frequent word
    • Vb - verb
  2. Number
    • Sg - singular
    • Dl - dual
    • Pl - plural
  3. Person
    • 1 - me, we
    • 2 - you
    • 3 - he/she/it, they
  4. Gender
    • Ms - masculine
    • Fm - feminine
    • Nt - neuter
  5. Specification
    • Detm - determined
    • Undt - undetermined
  6. Comparision
    • Post - positive (no comparision)
    • Cmpr - comparative
    • Supl - superlative
  7. Modal
    • L - local
    • T - temporal
    • M - modal
  8. Tempus/Verb form
    • Pr - present
    • Ip - imperfect
    • Pf - perfect
    • In - infinitive
    • Ge - gerundium
    • Pa - participe
    • Fu - future
    • C1 - conjunctive I, subjonctif
    • Pq - plusquamperfect
    • C2 - conjunctive II, subjonctif passé
    • Im - imperative
  9. Active/Passive
    • Actv - active
    • Pasv - passive
  10. Casus
    • Nomv - nominative
    • Genv - genitive
    • Datv - dative
    • Accv - accusative
    • Ablv - ablative
  11. Other grammatical modifiers
    • Anct - ancient, old form
    • Chan - chain of words (separated by underscores)
    • Colr - color
    • Diar - with diaresis (there is another word without)
    • Honr - honorative (personal pronouns in uppercase)
    • Prim - primary, elementary, not combined
    • Poss - possessive (pronoun)
    • Refl - reflexive (pronoun)
    • Relt - relative (pronoun)
    • Root - root form
    • Shor - shortened form, contraction
    • Strg - strong (e.g. verb)
    • Suff - suffix indicating some word type
    • Weak - weak (e.g. verb)
  12. Word Field
    • Bibl - words from the Holy Bible
    • Bota - Botanic, plants
    • Body - parts of (human) body
    • Card - cardinal: 0, 1, 2 ... (for numbers)
    • Caus - causal
    • Clth - cloth, weaving, texture
    • Curr - currency, money
    • Dial - Dialect, bad language
    • Frac - fractional: 1/2, 1/3 ... (for numbers)
    • Freq - indicates frequency
    • Forg - foreign (not native German) word or name, followed by an optional 2-letter ISO 636 country code
    • Geog - Geography
      • City - city or location name
      • Flum - river name (with gender)
      • Mont - mountain name
      These codes may be accompanied by Forg.
    • Jidd - Jiddish
    • Letr - Name of a (greek) letter
    • Locn - location
    • Meas - measurement unit
    • Medc - medicine, illness
    • Metl - metal
    • Meto - meteorology, wheather
    • Milt - military, weapon
    • Minr - mineralogy, juwelry
    • Modl - modal
    • Move - indicating (direction of) movement
    • Numr - related to some number
    • Nutr - nutrition, eatable or drinkable
    • Ordn - ordinal: 1st, 2nd ... (for numbers)
    • Orul - old writing rules (before 1996)
    • Pers - person
    • Pren - first name, christian name of a person
    • Prof - profession (implies person)
    • Phys - Physics
    • Ratn - rational number, fraction
    • Spor - sport, play
    • Surn - surname, last name of a person
    • Time - time related
      • Holy - name of holy day, sunday or holiday
      • Mnth - month name
      • Week - weekday
      • Seas - season
    • Zool - Zoology, animals
